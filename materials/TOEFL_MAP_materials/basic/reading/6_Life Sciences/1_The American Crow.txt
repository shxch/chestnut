The American Crow

The crow is a species of bird. It is typically black in color. Around the world, there are about forty species of crows. One of the most common of all crows is the American crow.

The crow lives almost everywhere in North America. It is about forty-five centimeters in length. Male crows are typically larger than females. Crows first breed when they are around four years of age. In spring, females lay up to six eggs. The eggs hatch after a gestation period of two and a half weeks. American crows eat virtually anything. They are often seen eating garbage and dead animals. They also eat wheat or corn in farmers' fields.

Studies of American crows indicate that they are highly intelligent. They are also more sociable than most birds. They produce loud cawing sounds. They use these sounds to communicate with one another.
